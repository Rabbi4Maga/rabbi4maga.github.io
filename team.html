Professor David Benarroch

is a proud American and traditionally observant conservative rabbi based in the United States. With a deep commitment to biblical truth and traditional values, he studied ancient manuscripts of the Old Testament in Israel under the mentorship of the late Rabbi Chaim Kanievsky, one of the most respected Torah scholars of his generation.

Professor Benarroch offers private lectures focused specifically on the Old Testament, exploring its history, language, and enduring relevance from a faith-based, conservative perspective. His teachings reflect a strong belief in Judeo-Christian values and the spiritual foundations that shaped both ancient Israel and modern Western civilization.

Lectures are available for $15 per hour. Perfect for Bible students, truth-seekers, and anyone who values faith, tradition, and the word of God.

Josef Hausenstein

is an accomplished educator specializing in both Hebrew and Aramaic. A proud American and committed conservative, he brings a deep respect for tradition and biblical values into his academic and teaching work.

He has extensively studied ancient and modern Hebrew, as well as classical Aramaic, with a focus on grammar, syntax, and literary tradition. His work includes the translation and analysis of numerous manuscripts discovered across the Middle East.

Josef earned his academic training in biblical languages at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, within the Faculty of Biblical and Ancient Near Eastern Studies, where he developed a strong foundation in philology and textual interpretation.
